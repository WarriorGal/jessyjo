Personal Manifesto

  • Calendars are essential. Organization is key. Dedicate time to get important things done to reduce or eliminate procrastination.
  • Be good to yourself. Eat and sleep everyday. Take time off when needed. See a doctor regularly. Understand that you are your own toughest critic.
  • New things aren’t scary. Even if you end up hating the new thing you just tried, you now know that this is something to steer clear of in the future.
  • You can do anything. Get started anywhere and you will end up somewhere.
  • Spend time doing things you like. Be around people who love you often. Have separate hobbies from your career.
  • Don’t obsess. Take a deep breath when you are feeling overwhelmed. Take breaks. Ask for second opinions.
  • Figure things out along the way. Don’t let not knowing how to do something stop you from doing it.
  • Iterate. Iterate. Iterate. Your first attempt looks like crap. Do it again.
  • Kindness goes a long way. People are willing to do more things for you if you are nice.
  • Change is okay. If you have had an idea for a long time that all of a sudden you feel differently about, embrace it. Change is personal growth.
